Karmak

The Karnak temple at Thebes (Amun-Ra) and Amenhotep’s temple on the western bank of the Nile River across from it are visual examples of the imagery used to impress the Egyptian people. Both temples are huge and contain massive statues. Amenhotep’s temple walls are twenty-six feet thick, eight hundred yards long, pained with bright colors, and decorated with statues at the entrances made from red granite, white alabaster, and quartzite.
